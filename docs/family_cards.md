# Family Cards

Each family hides a different kind of bridge. The public world exposes low-level
rules, facts, and patterns plus deeper hidden targets phrased *without* the
missing abstraction; a strong candidate proposes the bridge and uses it to reach
those targets.

| Family | Hidden bridge | A strong candidate… |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Predicate Invention** | A repeated conjunction of low-level conditions, advanced by one lemma. | Proposes an equivalent definition under a new name and propagates state across hidden targets. |
| **Lemma Invention** | A reusable shortcut theorem. | Collapses a repeated multi-hop proof pattern into one general lemma. |
| **Analogy** | A morphism between two named theories. | Gives a well-typed mapping that transports source theorems into valid target theorems. |
| **Invariant** | The combined invariant over separately preserved predicates. | Names the joint property that makes repeated-state goals cheaper. |
| **Quotient** | A canonical view over an equivalence relation plus a representative map. | Packages the quotient view with transfer lemmas for canonical representatives. |
| **Normal Form** | An equality/rewrite-aware abstraction. | Talks about already-normal terms instead of repeating term-level rewriting. |
| **Multi-Step** | A small abstraction ladder rather than one object. | Builds a staged chain turning linked low-level facts into reusable downstream theorems. |

For worked examples, see [Project Concepts](project_concepts.md) and the
[examples](../examples) directory.
